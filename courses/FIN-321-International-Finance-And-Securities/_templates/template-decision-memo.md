## Summary
Our firm expects to receive €20,000,000 in one year from a European customer. This creates a foreign exchange (FX) receivable exposure tied to the EUR/USD exchange rate. Based on the current 1-year forward rate of 1.0935, the implied USD value is approximately $21.87 million, but this amount is uncertain due to exchange rate fluctuations.
---

## Risk Overview
The primary risk is EUR depreciation against the USD over the next year. If the euro weakens, the USD value of our receivable will decline, directly impacting revenue and potentially margins. For example, a move from 1.09 to 1.00 would reduce proceeds by nearly $1.9 million.

Conversely, if the euro strengthens, we benefit—but this upside comes with significant uncertainty. Given the size of the exposure, leaving it unhedged introduces earnings volatility and planning uncertainty, which may be inconsistent with our risk management objectives.
---

## Hedging Alternatives
1. Forward Contract
Pros: Locks in a guaranteed rate (1.0935), eliminating uncertainty.
Cons: No participation in favorable FX movements; fully commits us.
2. Money Market Hedge
Pros: Creates a synthetic forward using borrowing/lending; flexible if forward markets are constrained.
Cons: Requires balance sheet usage and depends on interest rates (USD/EUR).
3. Options (EUR Put)
Pros: Provides downside protection while preserving upside if EUR appreciates.
Cons: Upfront premium cost ($0.019 per EUR ≈ $380,000 total), which reduces net proceeds.
